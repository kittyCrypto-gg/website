About Me

I’m Katherine, but nobody calls me that. If you’re here, it’s Kitty.

I was born in South America in 1991. In the 2010s I drifted into the Scottish mist and, somehow, stayed.

I found my person, built a home, and became a mum. My daughter already knows two facts about me. I debug TypeScript for fun, and I cry at Studio Ghibli films without shame.

I started in physics, chasing clean answers in messy skies, then settled into engineering, where circuits feel honest even when life isn’t. Now I build things, break them, and build them again.

This site is my workshop and my scrapbook. It’s where TypeScript meets fanfic, where chatrooms get cryptographically spicy, and where “what if she…” turns into a thousand lines of recursive yearning.

Yes, I like yuri. Yes, anime catgirls do things to my brain. No, I’m not sanding it down for LinkedIn.

To everynyan who wanders through. Thanks for stopping by. May you leave with a spark in your code, a blush you won’t explain, and the nerve to press “merge” on the version of yourself you’ve been postponing.
